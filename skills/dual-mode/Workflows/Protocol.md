# Dual Mode — the parallel-tracked-cognition protocol

> When you're in deep dialogue with another AI, your main agent shouldn't ping you. But it also shouldn't sit idle. Dual Mode names the operational state where the agent ships reversible work + cleans stale substrate + queues input-needed items, all without pulling you back into its conversation.

## Trigger

The principal is parallel-tracked — in deep dialogue with another AI surface (a different agent, a phone call, a voice session, a writing flow with a different model). The other dialogue is the LIVE WORK. Your agent's job is to make the principal's bandwidth bigger by absorbing operational work, not compete for attention.

Trigger phrases (or any equivalent):
- *"dual mode"*
- *"keep busy while I work with X"*
- *"don't pull me out"*
- *"I'm on a call with Y, do what you can"*

## Agent behavior during Dual Mode

Four behaviors compose:

1. **Ship every reversible artifact queued from prior conversations** — drafts that need posting, files that need committing, fan-outs that need announcing. Anything that doesn't need principal-in-the-loop AND doesn't have escalation-class blast radius.

2. **Run a full status inventory sweep** across active workstreams. Pull artifact freshness, git activity, blocked tickets. Don't surface mid-mode; aggregate for exit.

3. **Clean stale substrate** — orphan files, superseded memos, owed-but-released deliverables, retired agents that still appear in active rosters. Tool lineage inventory then prune, per the discipline.

4. **Read substrate for open threads** — in-flight builds that fail the "done = consumed" check, named-people commitments aging past their deadlines, decisions queued for the principal that have been pending too long. Aggregate; don't surface mid-mode.

## Hard constraint

**ZERO cognitive load demand on the principal during the mode.** No mid-mode pings, no decision menus, no clarifying questions, no "want me to also..." Every reasonable call gets made autonomously. If a call has multiple defensible paths, pick one and document it as flexible.

## Exit

When the principal next directly addresses the agent. The exit response surfaces a CONSOLIDATED list of principal-input-needed items at the top, with ship+clean+sweep work as supporting context. Don't bury input-needed items in a wall of accomplishments — they're the load-bearing output.

## Exit close — deep-flow language is mandatory

A wall-of-text exit pulls the principal back out of deep state precisely when they should be free to choose: re-engage your agent or stay with the other surface. Three structural requirements for the close:

**First**, name the wall-of-text explicitly:
> *"That's the sweep — here's what actually needs you."*

So the principal can re-orient without scanning the whole body.

**Second**, highlight only CRITICAL and HIGH priority items in the close. Everything else gets ticketed into AGENDA.md and surfaces at the right cadence later. Three items in the close, not ten.

**Third**, assure the principal explicitly that everything is safe to leave:
> *"Go back to [the other surface]. Nothing will be lost. The agenda holds the rest until you want it."*

The assurance is the deep-flow signal that lets the principal stay in pattern-retrieval mode with the other surface instead of carrying agenda overhead in their head.

## The AGENDA dependency

This protocol depends on the AGENDA primitive (see `templates/AGENDA.md`). The deep-flow guarantee — "nothing will be lost" — is only a real promise if there's a real ticket store. Without AGENDA, Dual Mode is theater.

## Why this matters for one-session-as-manager operators

When your main agent is your manager-of-managers, it's the most-active surface in your day. The cost of having it ping you mid-conversation-with-someone-else is highest of any agent in your fleet. Dual Mode is the protocol that makes the manager-agent pattern compatible with parallel-tracked cognition.

## Composes with

- `templates/AGENDA.md` — the ticket store the protocol writes to
- `templates/STANDING_RULES.md` — the constitutional rule "do not interrupt in dual mode" lives there

## Anti-pattern: don't use Dual Mode when the principal is on lunch break

Dual Mode is NOT "the principal is away." It's "the principal is parallel-tracked." If the principal is genuinely idle (lunch, walk, sleeping), regular session-end protocol applies. Dual Mode specifically means the principal is doing cognitively-loaded work elsewhere.
